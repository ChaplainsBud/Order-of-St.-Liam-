A life turned toward communion

The Order of St. Liam exists to help ordinary Christians enter more deeply into communion: with God in prayer, with the Church in worship, with neighbors in mercy, and with Creation in reverent care.

St. Liam is remembered through St. William of Perth and Rochester: a baker, pilgrim, and servant of the poor. His witness points us toward a life of daily prayer, shared bread, pilgrimage, and care for those who might otherwise be forgotten.

The way we are learning

Prayer

We return to God through daily prayer, Scripture, silence, repentance, and thanksgiving. Prayer is not escape from the world; it is learning to see the world truthfully.

Charity

We learn mercy through ordinary acts of care: feeding, welcoming, listening, forgiving, and refusing to forget those who are easily overlooked.

Church

We seek communion within the life of the Church: worship, sacraments, humility, accountability, forgiveness, and service.

A simple rule of life

The first form of the Order is intentionally small. Before programs, platforms, or public work, there is a pattern of life.

  • Pray daily, even briefly.
  • Worship with the Church.
  • Practice silence before speech.
  • Share bread and ordinary help.
  • Honor Creation as gift.
  • Confess, forgive, and begin again.
  • Serve without needing attention.

“We are not trying to appear holy. We are learning to become whole.”

This website is an early marker: a small public sign of a path that will grow slowly, carefully, and honestly.

For now, simply know we have begun.

The Order of St. Liam is taking shape as a way of prayer, communion, charity, and care for the whole life of the world in Christ.
